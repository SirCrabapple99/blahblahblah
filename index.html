Jadav Payeng

Jadav "Molai" Payeng is an enviornmental activist from India, well known as the 'Forest Man of India'. He was born in the Mising tribe of Assam.

When Jadav Payeng was 16, he found many dead snakes, which had been killed by the heat on the tree-less sandbar.

He then decided to plant bamboo and tree seedlings, in an effort to restore the area back to a beautiful forest.

30 years later, the forest is around 1300 acres large.

With the Molai Forest spanning around 1350 acres, it is approximately 1.6 times larger than Manhattans 842 square acre Central Park in New York!

That's around 1.6 times larger than Central Park!
In 2016, Jadav recieved the Padma Shri award, the fourth highest reward that citizens can recieve in india.

The Padma Shri award is given every year by the Government of India to recognize "distinguished contribution in various spheres of activity including the arts, education, industry, literature, science, acting, medicine, social service and public affairs"
